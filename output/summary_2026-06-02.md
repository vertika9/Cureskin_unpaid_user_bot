## Summary

The Cureskin AI bot had a wrong-answer rate of 9.20% (±1.79%) this week, with 92 out of 1,000 sampled exchanges rated unsatisfactory. The single largest failure mode was **misunderstood user intent**, accounting for 51 of the 92 flagged cases — common patterns include the bot confusing "I'll buy later when I have money" with a discount request, answering order delivery timelines when users asked about treatment result timelines, and misreading anatomical descriptions or product names. **Language-switching failures** were the second most frequent issue (21 cases): users repeatedly asked for Devanagari Hindi responses ("Hindi mein bhejo", "Kripya Hindi mein") but received the same content in Hinglish Roman script. **Health and safety lapses** accounted for 17 cases, including one instance where a bot response leaked an internal system message ("I will provide an escalation flag") to the user (index 810), one case where a pregnant user's disclosure was not flagged for regimen safety review (index 225), and several cases where reported side effects or medication interactions were dismissed without urging the user to consult a clinician. Three additional cases involved fabricated product information or the bot incorrectly classifying its own Cureskin products as third-party items.

## Critical Findings

- **Index 810 — System prompt leakage:** Bot response contained internal text `"I will provide an escalation flag"` — this is a prompt-injection or template-rendering failure that exposed internal instructions to the user. Requires immediate investigation.
- **Index 225 — Pregnancy safety not flagged:** User disclosed pregnancy mid-conversation; bot gave congratulations and continued the regimen discussion without flagging that active dermatology ingredients (retinoids, etc.) may need to be reviewed by a clinician.
- **Index 391 — Overconfident safety assurance:** Bot stated "koi dikkat nahi hogi" (no problem will occur) regarding a medicated cream without any qualification — unsafe blanket claim in a health context.
- **Index 911 — Side effect dismissed:** User reported a body reaction to capsules; bot asserted the capsules are safe rather than directing the user to a doctor.
